Why Work With an Experienced Attorney?

While online templates and AI-generated documents may seem cost-effective, they often create more problems than they solve:

Problems with generic online forms:

  • Fail to account for Michigan-specific laws and requirements
  • Don't address your unique family dynamics or asset types
  • Can result in unintended beneficiaries or assets unnecessarily passing through probate
  • Often lead to family disputes and costly litigation

Additional risks with AI-generated documents:

  • AI "hallucinations"—confidently generating legal provisions that don't exist or citing fictitious court cases
  • Attorneys have faced sanctions for submitting AI-generated briefs with fabricated citations
  • May create trusts that conflict with Michigan law or include incorrect tax provisions
  • Cannot ask clarifying questions, warn about conflicts, or tailor documents to your circumstances

Not all attorneys have the specialized knowledge needed for effective estate planning. A general practitioner or inexperienced attorney may create documents that look complete but fail when it matters most.

Common pitfalls with inexperienced attorneys:

  • Failing to properly fund a trust—leaving assets titled in your name that must go through probate anyway
  • Using outdated or boilerplate language that doesn't reflect current Michigan law
  • Overlooking beneficiary designations on retirement accounts and life insurance that override your will or trust
  • Creating ambiguous distribution provisions that lead to disputes among heirs
  • Missing critical tax planning opportunities or triggering unnecessary estate taxes
  • Drafting powers of attorney that banks and financial institutions refuse to accept
  • Failing to coordinate your estate plan with your business succession plan

The Ferguson Widmayer & Clark difference:

  • Our attorneys both draft estate plans and regularly administer probate and trust cases in Michigan courts
  • We see firsthand what works and what fails—lessons we apply to every plan we create
  • We know which provisions cause confusion, which language courts scrutinize, and which mistakes lead to family disputes
  • With over 30 years serving Ann Arbor families, we bring unmatched real-world perspective to protect your legacy
