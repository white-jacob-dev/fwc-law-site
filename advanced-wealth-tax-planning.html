Advanced Wealth & Tax Planning

Sophisticated strategies to preserve wealth, minimize taxes, and secure your family's financial future.

View FAQs

For individuals and families with significant assets, standard estate planning may not be sufficient. Ferguson Widmayer & Clark PC offers advanced wealth and tax planning strategies designed to protect, preserve, and transfer wealth across generations while minimizing tax exposure.

Current Federal Gift & Estate Tax Exemptions (2026)

  • Annual Gift Tax Exclusion: $19,000 per recipient (you can give this amount to as many people as you wish each year without using any of your lifetime exemption)
  • Lifetime Estate/Gift Tax Exemption (Individual): $13.99 million per person
  • Lifetime Estate/Gift Tax Exemption (Married Couple): $27.98 million combined (with portability)

These figures are adjusted annually by the IRS. Consult with our attorneys about how current exemption levels affect your planning.

Advanced Trust Strategies

Sophisticated trust structures can provide powerful tools for wealth preservation, asset protection, and tax minimization. We design and implement trusts tailored to your specific goals and circumstances.

  • Irrevocable Life Insurance Trusts (ILITs): Remove life insurance proceeds from your taxable estate while providing liquidity for estate taxes and other needs.
  • Grantor Retained Annuity Trusts (GRATs): Transfer appreciation to heirs with minimal gift tax consequences.
  • Qualified Personal Residence Trusts (QPRTs): Transfer your home to beneficiaries at a reduced gift tax value while retaining the right to live there.
  • Charitable Remainder Trusts: Generate income, support charitable causes, and achieve significant tax benefits.
  • Dynasty Trusts: Preserve wealth for multiple generations while minimizing transfer taxes.
  • Spousal Lifetime Access Trusts (SLATs): Remove assets from your estate while maintaining indirect access through your spouse.

Gift & Estate Tax Planning

Strategic use of gift and estate tax exemptions can significantly reduce the tax burden on wealth transfers. We help clients maximize the benefit of current exemption levels and plan for potential future changes.

  • Lifetime Gifting Strategies: Leveraging annual exclusions and lifetime exemptions to transfer wealth tax-efficiently.
  • Valuation Discounts: Structuring transfers of business interests and other assets to take advantage of appropriate discounts.
  • Generation-Skipping Transfer Planning: Strategies to minimize GST taxes when transferring wealth to grandchildren and beyond.
  • Portability Planning: Maximizing the use of both spouses' estate tax exemptions.
  • Charitable Giving Strategies: Donor-advised funds, private foundations, and other vehicles for philanthropic goals.

Income Tax Planning

Effective income tax planning works hand-in-hand with estate planning to preserve more of your wealth. We coordinate strategies that address both current income and long-term wealth transfer goals.

  • Trust Income Tax Planning: Structuring trusts for optimal income tax treatment.
  • Basis Planning: Strategies to maximize stepped-up basis at death and minimize capital gains.
  • Retirement Account Planning: Tax-efficient strategies for IRAs, 401(k)s, and other qualified plans.
  • Charitable Deduction Optimization: Timing and structuring charitable gifts for maximum tax benefit.

Family Wealth Governance

Preserving wealth across generations requires more than legal documents—it requires thoughtful planning for how wealth will be managed and used by future generations.

  • Family Limited Partnerships & LLCs: Structures for managing family assets and facilitating wealth transfers.
  • Family Governance Documents: Mission statements, policies, and procedures for family wealth management.
  • Next Generation Planning: Preparing heirs to responsibly manage inherited wealth.
  • Incentive Trust Provisions: Encouraging education, career achievement, and responsible behavior.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about advanced wealth and tax planning

The answers provided are for informational purposes only and do not constitute legal advice. Every situation is unique. Please schedule a consultation with one of our attorneys to discuss your specific circumstances.

An irrevocable trust is a trust that generally cannot be modified or terminated without the court's permission or without the permission of the beneficiaries (in limited circumstances) once established. Unlike revocable trusts, assets in an irrevocable trust are generally removed from your taxable estate, providing estate tax benefits. They can also offer asset protection, Medicaid planning benefits, and allow you to leverage gift tax exemptions. Common types include life insurance trusts, charitable trusts, and generation-skipping trusts.

Estate tax reduction strategies include making annual exclusion gifts ($19,000 per recipient in 2026), using your lifetime gift/estate tax exemption during life, establishing irrevocable trusts to remove assets from your estate, charitable giving strategies, family limited partnerships for valuation discounts, and life insurance planning. The key is starting early—many strategies work best over time and may be unavailable or less effective if implemented too late.

A family limited partnership (FLP) is an entity that allows family members to pool assets—often investments or real estate—while senior generations maintain control as general partners. Limited partnership interests can be gifted to children at discounted values due to lack of control and marketability. FLPs provide centralized asset management, potential creditor protection, and can facilitate gradual wealth transfer while parents retain control.

Charitable trusts come in two main forms. A Charitable Remainder Trust (CRT) pays income to you or your beneficiaries for life or a term of years, with the remainder going to charity—providing an immediate tax deduction and income stream. A Charitable Lead Trust (CLT) pays income to charity first, with the remainder passing to your heirs at reduced gift/estate tax cost. Both can help achieve philanthropic goals while providing significant tax benefits.

For 2026, the federal estate tax exemption is approximately $13.99 million per person. However, this exemption is subject to change based on legislation. It's important to consult with our attorneys about current exemption levels and plan accordingly, as strategic lifetime gifting can help maximize the use of available exemptions before potential future reductions.
