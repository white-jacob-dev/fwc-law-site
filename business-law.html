Real Estate Law

Real estate transactions involve significant financial commitments and complex legal considerations. Our attorneys guide clients through every aspect of property matters, ensuring your interests are protected.

  • Residential Transactions: Purchase agreements, title review, closing representation, and resolving title issues.
  • Commercial Real Estate: Acquisitions, sales, lease negotiations, and development agreements.
  • Land Use & Zoning: Navigating zoning regulations, variance applications, and land use approvals.
  • Title Issues: Resolving liens, encumbrances, boundary disputes, and easement matters.
  • Property Transfers: Deeds, land contracts, and transfers within estate plans or business structures.

Business Law

From formation to ongoing operations, we provide strategic business counsel that helps you navigate challenges, seize opportunities, and build lasting success.

  • Business Formation: Entity selection and formation for LLCs, corporations, partnerships, and other structures.
  • Operating Agreements & Bylaws: Governance documents tailored to your business needs and ownership structure.
  • Contract Drafting & Review: Commercial agreements, vendor contracts, licensing arrangements, and partnership agreements.
  • Buy-Sell Agreements: Protecting business continuity through well-structured ownership transition plans.
  • Mergers & Acquisitions: Due diligence, negotiation, and documentation for buying or selling businesses.
  • Business Disputes: Resolution of contract disputes, partnership disagreements, and commercial conflicts.

Tax Law

Sound tax planning is essential to protecting your wealth and achieving your financial objectives. We work with clients to develop tax-efficient strategies across personal and business matters.

  • Tax Planning: Strategic planning to minimize tax liability while achieving personal and business goals.
  • Entity Tax Structure: Selecting and structuring business entities for optimal tax treatment.
  • Real Estate Tax Issues: 1031 exchanges, property tax appeals, and tax implications of real estate transactions.
  • Gift & Estate Tax Planning: Strategies to minimize transfer taxes and preserve wealth for future generations.
  • Tax Controversy: Representation in audits, appeals, and disputes with taxing authorities.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about real estate, business, and tax law in Michigan

The answers provided are for informational purposes only and do not constitute legal advice. Every situation is unique. Please schedule a consultation with one of our attorneys to discuss your specific circumstances.

While Michigan does not legally require an attorney at closing, having legal representation is highly advisable. An attorney can review the purchase agreement, examine the title commitment for issues, ensure all documents are properly prepared, and protect your interests at closing. This is especially important for complex transactions, commercial properties, or when issues arise during the process.

The best entity depends on your specific situation. LLCs offer flexibility and pass-through taxation with liability protection. S-Corps can provide tax savings on self-employment taxes but have restrictions on ownership. C-Corps offer unlimited growth potential but face double taxation. Factors to consider include number of owners, tax implications, liability concerns, and growth plans. We recommend consulting with both an attorney and accountant to make this decision.

A 1031 exchange allows you to defer capital gains taxes when selling investment or business property by reinvesting the proceeds into a "like-kind" property. You must identify replacement property within 45 days and complete the purchase within 180 days. A qualified intermediary must hold the funds during the exchange. This powerful tool can help you grow your real estate portfolio while deferring significant tax obligations.

Business succession to children can be accomplished through several strategies: gifting shares over time using annual exclusions, selling the business with an installment note, creating a family limited partnership or LLC, or transferring through a trust. The best approach depends on your goals for retirement income, tax minimization, control retention, and treatment of children active vs. inactive in the business. Early planning is essential.

Essential contracts for most small businesses include: client/customer service agreements, vendor and supplier contracts, employment agreements and offer letters, independent contractor agreements, non-disclosure agreements (NDAs), operating agreement or bylaws, and lease agreements if renting space. The specific contracts needed depend on your industry and business model. Having proper agreements in place protects your business from disputes and liability.
