Probate, Estate Settlement & Trust Administration

Guiding families through the estate settlement process with sensitivity and efficiency.

Losing a loved one is difficult enough without the added burden of navigating complex legal proceedings. At Ferguson Widmayer & Clark PC, we guide families through probate and estate administration with compassion, clarity, and attention to detail.

Probate Administration

Probate is the legal process of settling a deceased person's estate under court supervision. Whether formal or informal proceedings are required, we help personal representatives fulfill their duties efficiently and in compliance with Michigan law.

  • Opening the Estate: Filing necessary petitions, publishing notice to creditors, and securing appointment of the personal representative.
  • Asset Inventory: Identifying, valuing, and documenting all estate assets.
  • Creditor Claims: Reviewing, negotiating, and paying valid claims against the estate.
  • Tax Compliance: Preparing and filing required estate, inheritance, and income tax returns.
  • Distribution: Transferring assets to beneficiaries in accordance with the will or state law.
  • Court Accounting: Preparing detailed accountings for court approval.

Trust Administration

When a loved one passes away with a trust, the successor trustee assumes significant responsibilities. We help trustees understand and fulfill their fiduciary duties while navigating the administration process.

  • Trustee Guidance: Explaining fiduciary duties, investment standards, and distribution requirements.
  • Asset Management: Assisting with retitling assets, managing investments, and maintaining proper records.
  • Beneficiary Communications: Preparing required notices and accountings for beneficiaries.
  • Tax Planning: Coordinating with tax professionals on trust income, estate, and generation-skipping taxes.
  • Distributions: Implementing distribution provisions according to trust terms.

Estate Litigation

When disputes arise over wills, trusts, or estate administration, our attorneys provide experienced representation to protect your interests.

  • Will Contests: Challenging or defending wills based on capacity, undue influence, or improper execution.
  • Trust Disputes: Addressing disagreements over trust interpretation, administration, or modification.
  • Breach of Fiduciary Duty: Pursuing or defending claims against personal representatives or trustees.
  • Beneficiary Rights: Enforcing or protecting the rights of estate and trust beneficiaries.

Guardianship & Conservatorship

When individuals can no longer make decisions for themselves, guardianship or conservatorship may be necessary. We help families navigate these proceedings with sensitivity to the protected person's dignity and best interests.

  • Guardianship: Obtaining authority to make personal and medical decisions for incapacitated adults or minors.
  • Conservatorship: Securing authority to manage financial affairs for those who cannot do so themselves.
  • Ongoing Compliance: Preparing annual reports and accountings required by the court.
