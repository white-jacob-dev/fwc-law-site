Virginia A. Cardwell

Virginia joined Ferguson Widmayer & Clark PC in October 2015 and focuses her practice on estate planning, estate and trust administration, and advanced wealth and tax planning.

Virginia received her undergraduate degree from the University of Michigan with Honors in 1989, and graduated from the University of Michigan Law School in 1992. Her diverse legal background includes experience in contracts, business matters, and family law, giving her a well-rounded perspective that enhances her estate planning practice.

Teaching & Education

Virginia has also been an instructor at Washtenaw Community College, where she has taught courses on Wills and Trusts. Her commitment to legal education reflects her dedication to helping others understand the importance of proper estate planning and the legal instruments that protect families and their assets.

Estate Planning Approach

With her extensive experience in transactional matters, Virginia brings a practical perspective to estate planning. She understands how plans are implemented and, when necessary, challenged, which informs her careful and thorough approach to drafting estate planning documents that will stand the test of time.
