Our Story

A legacy of excellence in legal practice, built on trust and dedication to our clients.

An Historic Home for Legal Excellence

Our firm was founded on a simple principle: that exceptional legal counsel begins with genuinely understanding each client's unique circumstances, goals, and concerns. This philosophy has guided us through generations of practice and remains at the heart of everything we do.

At Ferguson Widmayer & Clark PC, we combine deep legal expertise with a genuine commitment to building lasting relationships with our clients. We don't believe in one-size-fits-all solutions. Instead, we take the time to understand the complete picture—your family dynamics, financial situation, business interests, and personal goals—to develop strategies that truly serve your needs.

Our attorneys bring decades of combined experience across estate planning, business law, and probate matters. This breadth of knowledge allows us to provide comprehensive counsel that addresses all aspects of our clients' legal needs, often anticipating issues before they arise.

The Henry Cornwell House

Built in 1896

Our offices are located at 538 North Division Street in Ann Arbor, in an historic 1896 Victorian home that reflects our firm's commitment to tradition, quality, and enduring service. The beautifully preserved architecture features elegant woodwork, ornate staircases, and period details that speak to our appreciation for craftsmanship and lasting quality.

The home was built by Henry Cornwell, who along with his brother Harvey, owned a paper mill on the Huron River near the Barton Dam. The Queen Anne/Colonial Revival house replaced an earlier Italianate house, which had stood on the same site. The 17-room house was one of several homes highlighted in the 1896 Ann Arbor Headlight. After Henry died, his son Frank and his family remained in the home until World War I, when it became too large for the family. It went through several subsequent owners including the Beth Israel Congregation, the Pentecostal Church of God, St. Thomas Church, and several private owners and businesses. Ferguson Widmayer & Clark has occupied the building since 1998.

Source: Historic Buildings, Ann Arbor, MI by Marjorie Reade and Susan Wineberg, copyright 1982.
